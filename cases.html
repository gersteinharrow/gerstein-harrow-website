Plaintiff-Side Crypto Litigation

Houghton v. Compound DAO

N.D. Cal. · 2022 Class Action · Securities

Class action against Compound DAO and its founders, venture investors, and governance participants for securities law violations. We developed theories holding the founders and investors of a decentralized protocol personally liable for investor losses — an approach that got real traction in court. The court denied the motion to dismiss.

Complaint Order Denying MTD

Sarcuni v. bZx DAO

S.D. Cal. · 2022 Class Action · DAO Liability

bZx was a crypto lending protocol whose founders promised users their funds were safe from hacks. When hackers drained tens of millions in user deposits, we filed a class action against the DAO, its founders, and its investors. The case was one of the first to hold DAO participants accountable for protocol failures — a legal theory courts ultimately sustained.

Complaint

Samuels v. Lido DAO

N.D. Cal. · 2023 Class Action · Securities

Lido DAO runs one of the world's largest Ethereum staking businesses and issued LDO tokens — which we argue are unregistered securities. The case presents genuinely novel questions: Can a DAO be sued? Who are its partners? When does a software protocol cross the line into an investment contract? The court called these "new and important questions" and denied motions to dismiss.

Complaint MTD Opinion

Celacare Technologies v. Circle Internet Financial

D. Mass. · 2024 UCC · Stablecoins

Our client lost $1 million in USDC after a typo sent the coins to an unreachable Ethereum address. We're arguing that USDC are "financial assets" under UCC Article 8 — which would require Circle, the issuer, to honor or reissue the coins. It's a genuinely novel legal question with real stakes for anyone who uses stablecoins.

Novel Asset Recovery for Victims of Terror

Kim et al. v. Democratic People's Republic of Korea

S.D.N.Y. · 2025 Judgment Enforcement · Crypto Garnishment

After winning a $330 million federal court judgment against North Korea for the murder of their family member, the Kim family pursued garnishment of USDC held by Circle Internet Financial that investigators traced to North Korea. This involves novel questions about how international judgments can be enforced against crypto assets held by a U.S. financial company.

Kim et al. v. United States (USDT Forfeiture)

D.D.C. · 2025–26 Asset Forfeiture · Victims' Rights

The U.S. government seized approximately $14 million in USDT linked to North Korea. We intervened in the forfeiture proceeding on behalf of the Kim family — who hold federal court judgments against North Korea for the kidnapping and murder of their father — arguing that the terror victims, not the government, are entitled to those assets.

Claimant's Claim Motion for Judgment

Kim et al. v. Railgun DAO

D.D.C. · 2026 Anti-Terrorism Act · DAO Liability

Terror attack victims — including the family of Reverend Dong Shik Kim, a U.S. citizen kidnapped and murdered by North Korea, and survivors of Hezbollah's 2006 rocket campaign in northern Israel — sued Railgun DAO, an anonymous cryptocurrency mixer. North Korea and Hezbollah used Railgun's protocol to launder hundreds of millions in stolen crypto to fund their operations. The case asks whether a DAO built to enable financial anonymity can be held civilly liable under the Anti-Terrorism Act for the terrorism it enables.

Civil Rights

John Doe v. New Mexico Law Offices of the Public Defender

N.M. 1st Dist. & Ct. App. · 2024–25 Right to Counsel

In 2011, John Doe was arrested as a minor in New Mexico. His public defender allowed him to plead guilty under terms that clearly required his release by age 21 — but no one enforced that limit. A second attorney appointed to help him get out made the same mistake. Doe spent over a decade in prison past his lawful release date. We sued the New Mexico public defender system for violating his constitutional right to counsel, and are now pursuing the appeal.

Complaint Appellate Brief

Proton Associates v. Avelo Airlines

D. Nev. · 2025 First Amendment · Parody

Our client leased two billboards reading "AvelNO" to protest Avelo Airlines' contract to fly deportation flights for ICE. The word "AvelNO" is a parody of Avelo's trademark, with a red "N" inserted. Rather than answer the criticism, Avelo sued for trademark infringement. We're defending his right to protest — and to use parody to do it.

Complex Issues & Appeals

Javed v. Experis US, Inc. & IBM

Cal. Ct. App. · 2022–23 PAGA · Labor · Appeal

A PAGA representative action brought on behalf of thousands of California workers allegedly misclassified as independent contractors by staffing giant Experis (a ManpowerGroup subsidiary) and IBM. The case raised complex questions about PAGA standing, joint employer liability, and classification standards. We briefed and argued the appeal in the California First Appellate District.

Opening Brief Court of Appeal Opinion

Nomorobo v. Harris & Harris, Ltd.

N.D. Tex. · 2025 TCPA · Robocalls · Novel Standing

Nomorobo is a robocall-blocking service that operates roughly 290,000 "honeypot" phone numbers — lines that exist solely to catch and identify spam callers. Harris & Harris, a debt collector, called those numbers over 16,554 times using illegal automated dialing equipment, costing Nomorobo between $600,000 and $1 million annually. We're litigating a novel question: can a business entity whose entire purpose is to receive and catalog illegal calls sue under the TCPA?

A sample of our current and recent docket. This isn't everything — just a representative look at the kinds of cases we work on.

Crypto Consumer Protection

Houghton v. Compound DAO

N.D. Cal. · 2022 Class Action

Class action against Compound DAO and its founders, venture investors, and governance participants for securities law violations. We developed theories holding the founders and investors of a decentralized protocol personally liable for investor losses — an approach that got real traction in court.

Celacare Technologies v. Circle Internet Financial

D. Mass. · 2024 UCC · Stablecoins

Our client, a Texas tech company, lost $1 million in USDC after a typo sent the coins to an address nobody can reach on the Ethereum network. We're arguing that USDC are "financial assets" under UCC Article 8, which would require Circle — the issuer — to honor or reissue the coins. It's a genuinely novel legal question with real stakes for anyone who uses stablecoins.

Kim et al. v. Democratic People's Republic of Korea

S.D.N.Y. · 2025 Enforcement

Seeking garnishment of funds held by Circle Internet Financial as part of enforcement efforts against North Korea. This involves novel questions about how international judgments can be enforced against crypto assets held by a U.S. financial company.

Civil Rights & First Amendment

Proton Associates v. Avelo Airlines

D. Nev. · 2025 Parody · Trademark

Our client leased two billboards reading "AvelNO" to protest Avelo Airlines' contract to fly deportation flights for ICE. The word "AvelNO" is a parody of Avelo's trademark, with a red "N" inserted. Avelo responded with a trademark lawsuit instead of answering his criticism. We're defending his right to do exactly that.

Consumer Protection

Nomorobo v. Harris & Harris, Ltd.

N.D. Tex. · 2025 TCPA · Robocalls

Nomorobo is a service that blocks robocalls by maintaining roughly 290,000 phone numbers that exist to catch and identify spam callers. Harris & Harris, a debt collector, called those numbers over 16,554 times using illegal automated methods — costing Nomorobo between $600,000 and $1 million a year. We're suing for statutory damages and an injunction.
